            Advanced Iterative Fourier Algorithms
         ‚Ä¢ EST is one type of iterative Fourier algorithm
                ‚Ä¢ Acquire data on a linogram grid to allow direct, iterative Fourier inversion
                ‚Ä¢ Incorporate constraints into reconstruction (all voxels > 0)
                ‚Ä¢ Attempts to calculate the best match between the reconstruction and
                  experimental data
         ‚Ä¢ Other newer algorithms provide similar or even improved fidelity of
           the reconstruction
                ‚Ä¢ GENFIRE: angle requirement is relaxed and allows for optimized angular
                  alignment (http://www.physics.ucla.edu/research/imaging/FePt/)
                ‚Ä¢ Compressed sensing: improved reconstruction from noisy or fewer
                  projections. Requires a sparsity condition:
                   ‚Ä¢ L1- or L2-norm penalization (differences or square differences)
                   ‚Ä¢ Total variation minimization (enforcing smoothness but edge preserving)
