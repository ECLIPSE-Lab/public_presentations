# Unit 4 Plan — Probability Foundations (MFML)

## Unit intent
Establish the probabilistic language required for inference, uncertainty-aware modeling, and risk-sensitive decisions in machine learning.

## Learning objectives
1. Use conditional probability, total probability, and Bayes theorem correctly.
2. Distinguish random variables, distributions, expectation, variance, and covariance.
3. Connect probabilistic concepts to ML tasks (classification, confidence, calibration).
4. Avoid common probabilistic reasoning traps (base-rate neglect, wrong conditioning).

## Dependency links
- Backward: Unit 2 (LA), Unit 3 (optimization framing)
- Forward: Unit 5 (random variables/distributions deepening), Unit 6 (inference)

## Slide architecture (50)
- 1–10: Foundations (spaces, events, axioms, counting)
- 11–20: Conditional probability and Bayes in ML contexts
- 21–34: Random variables, distributions, moments, LLN/CLT intuition
- 35–44: ML implications (likelihood, calibration, decision thresholds)
- 45–50: Summary + exercises + references

## Exercise scaffold
- E1: Conditional probability computation with event tables
- E2: Bayes/base-rate scenario analysis
- E3: Distribution selection and interpretation for one ML variable
