1. The Long History of Data

Data is as old as civilization

  • 3400 BCE: Sumerian cuneiform for counting sheep and grain.
  • The first Metadata: Symbols representing not just values, but context (units, objects) Sandfeld, Stefan et al., (2024).
  • Antikythera Mechanism: Analog computation for celestial data.

Kepler: The First Data Analyst

  • 1609: Kepler inherited 25 years of Mars observations from Tycho Brahe.
  • He didn’t invent a new telescope; he invented a data-driven explanation.
  • Transition from descriptive (circles) to explanatory (ellipses) models.

2. What is Materials Data Science?

The Interdisciplinary Intersection

MDS sits at the center of: - Machine Learning: Algorithms that learn patterns. - Statistics: Quantifying uncertainty and significance. - Computer Science: Handling large data volumes. - Domain Knowledge: The “Physics” that keeps ML grounded Sandfeld, Stefan et al., (2024).

Models are imitations of reality

“A model is a simplified imitation of a process or system that allows us to understand, predict, or control its behavior.” Neuer, Michael et al., (2024)

4. Why Materials ML is “Hard”

The Unique Challenges

  1. Small Data / High Cost: A single TEM experiment can cost thousands of Euros.
  2. Multi-Modal: Fusing micrographs (2D/3D) with spectra (1D/4D).
  3. Measurement Noise: Physical artifacts (drift, charging) vs. actual signal.
  4. The Curse of Dimensionality:
    • If you have 10 parameters, a simple grid search with 10 values each requires \(10^{10}\) experiments!
    • Materials spaces are high-dimensional but sparsely sampled Sandfeld, Stefan et al., (2024).

Parsimony & Occam’s Razor

“Among competing hypotheses, the one with the fewest assumptions should be selected.” McClarren, Ryan G., (2021)

6. Supervised vs. Unsupervised Learning

Supervised (Learning with a Teacher)

  • Regression: Predicting a continuous value (e.g., Yield Strength).
  • Classification: Predicting a discrete label (e.g., Phase: FCC vs. BCC).

Unsupervised (Finding Hidden Patterns)

  • Clustering: Grouping similar spectra.
  • Dimensionality Reduction: Visualizing high-dimensional micrographs in 2D (PCA/t-SNE).

7. The Workflow: CRISP-DM for Labs

Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining

  1. Business (Scientific) Understanding: What is the question?
  2. Data Understanding: Inspecting for noise and artifacts.
  3. Data Preparation: Scaling, Normalization, Cleaning.
  4. Modeling: Selecting the right “Grey-Box”.
  5. Evaluation: Does it generalize to new batches?
  6. Deployment: Real-time control in the lab.

8. Correlation vs. Causality

The “Ice Cream” Trap

  • Correlation: Ice cream sales and crime rates both go up in summer.
  • Spurious Correlation: Ice cream does not cause crime. Heat is the hidden variable.

In Materials Science

  • Does a specific element cause strength, or is it a proxy for a specific grain size?
  • We must use the PSPP graph to distinguish causal links from noise Neuer, Michael et al., (2024).

9. Summary & Handoff

Top Takeaways
