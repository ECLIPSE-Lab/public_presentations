# Materials Genomics Unit 2 — 50-Slide Teaching Scaffold (book-backed)

## Book-backed content summary (for this unit)
- Materials datasets are generated by simulations whose assumptions define both the signal and the bias seen by downstream ML.
- FEM, MD, MC, and DFT produce fundamentally different output objects, from continuum fields to trajectories to total energies and electronic observables.
- Numerical settings, exchange-correlation choices, force fields, and sampling logic act as hidden confounders if they are stripped from the metadata.
- Students need to understand not just what quantity is stored in a database, but how it was produced and at what fidelity.
- The unit prepares later database and representation units by making simulation provenance an explicit part of the learning problem.
